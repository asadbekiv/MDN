Types of whales are grouped into 8 families, all fully-adapted to living in the water.

  1. As cetaceans, whales are in the same group as dolphins and porpoises
  2. Whales are descendants of the Artiodactyl, a land-living dinosaur species
  3. A whale could weigh from 500 pounds to 200 tons, depending on the type
  4. As cetaceans, whales are in the same group as dolphins and porpoises
  5. Most baleen whales have either visible hair or hair follicles
  6. Whale hair follicles have the same location as whiskers on terrestrial mammals
  7. Hunters catch whales for their meat and bones, and for medicinal purposes
